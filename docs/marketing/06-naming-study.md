# Naming Study — replacing "CommutePilot"

Date: July 2026. Builds on `docs/marketing/03-positioning-pricing.md` §4, which rules out
"-pilot"/"Co-" morphemes and "AI [noun]" category framing, and sketches six naming
directions. This study generates candidates across those directions (plus new ones),
runs live availability checks, and recommends a winner.

**Scope disclaimer (applies to every candidate below):** trademark checks here are quick
web scans for *obvious* conflicts, not a USPTO TESS clearance search or legal opinion.
A formal clearance search on the chosen name is a required pre-launch task (per
03-positioning §4 and Implication 9).

**Method / evidence key:**
- **App Store**: live iTunes Search API queries (`media=software` and `media=podcast`),
  July 7, 2026.
- **Domains**: live RDAP lookups via `rdap.org/domain/<name>.<tld>` for `.com`, `.app`,
  `.fm`. HTTP **404 = unregistered (available)**, 200 = registered. The `.fm` RDAP path
  was validated against known-registered controls (`tangent.fm`, `magpie.fm`, `bento.fm`
  all returned 200 with registrar data), so `.fm` 404s are genuine availability, not a
  TLD-coverage gap.
- Selection criteria: 1–2 syllables ideal; survives "Hey Siri, tell ___ to skip";
  spellable on first hearing; curiosity/curation-flavored; not commute-anchored; not
  AI-generic.

## 4. Recommendation

### Winner: **Foray** (product), **foray.fm** (domain)

Foray is the only candidate that scores at the top on soul *and* clears every hard gate.
The word means a brief venture into unfamiliar territory — it is the stretch card, the
"splatter," and the bounded session in a single two-syllable English word, with zero
commute anchoring and zero AI-generic residue. It makes the product's most defensible
claims speakable: "take a foray" is an invitation a sharp friend would make, and a foray
is *short by definition* — the anti-infinite-scroll promise is baked into the semantics.
"Hey Siri, tell Foray to skip" reads cleanly. Verb-ability is free marketing ("today's
foray," "foray a little further"). Evidence: App Store software search returns only
three small, unrelated apps (an agoraphobia tracker, a trail-GPS app, an indie game) —
**no podcast/audio/curation app named Foray exists**; podcast search finds only a
7-episode 2024 indie show ("FORAY," Robbie Stout) — no notable collision; **RDAP for
foray.fm returned HTTP 404 (unregistered) on July 7, 2026**, with the 404 validated
against registered .fm controls; foray.com/.app are taken by Foray Technologies
(forensic evidence software for law enforcement) — a distant class unlikely to conflict
with a consumer audio app, though formal clearance must confirm. Known residual risks:
the .com is not gettable (acceptable — .fm is category-native for audio, cf. anchor.fm,
transistor.fm), and a Canadian cannabis brand also uses "Foray" (different class).

### Runner-up 1: **Whim** (whim.fm)

The purest serendipity word in English, one syllable, unmissable spelling, and
**whim.fm returned RDAP 404 (unregistered) on July 7, 2026**. No podcast named exactly
"Whim" exists, and the best-known prior app collision — Whim, MaaS Global's
mobility-as-a-service app — went bankrupt with the app shut down in March 2024, so the
mark is fading rather than growing. Why it isn't the winner: the App Store is crowded
with live Whim-named apps (Whim Social, Whim AI Photo & Video, WhimIt AI Planner —
none in audio, but enough to blunt distinctiveness and complicate search), and "whim"
captures only the serendipity half of the soul — it says delightfully random more than
it says *knows you and explains the bridge*, which is the product's actual
differentiator.

### Runner-up 2: **Tangent**

The best pure soul-fit after Foray: it takes the exact behavior the product celebrates —
following a legible tangent off your main line of interest — and wears it as the brand.
App Store software search is effectively clear (a golf caddie, a Japanese e-signature
tool, a math calculator; nothing in audio/curation), and no major podcast owns the bare
name (several small "Tangent" shows exist, largest a niche proptech interview series).
Why it isn't the winner: **no domain is available — tangent.com, tangent.app, and
tangent.fm all returned RDAP 200 (registered)** — so shipping it means a compromise
domain (tangent.audio, gettangent.com) or an acquisition, and the small-podcast
namespace is noisier than Foray's. Keep it as the fallback if formal clearance
surprises us on Foray.

**Also-notable:** Fourcast (from the positioning doc) has fourcast.fm genuinely
available (RDAP 404) and cleverly encodes the four-card menu, but it fails two hard
criteria: it's a homophone of "forecast" (unspellable on first hearing, unsearchable),
and **"The Fourcast" is Channel 4 News's podcast (251 episodes)** — precisely the
podcast-app/podcast-name confusion this study was told to screen out.

## 5. How to secure it

1. **Register foray.fm now** (and whim.fm as the fallback — combined carrying cost is
   trivial vs. losing them). `.fm` is a premium ccTLD (Micronesia, operated by BRS
   Media): expect roughly **$70–100/yr** at Porkbun or Namecheap, sometimes more at
   first-year promo variance. Cloudflare Registrar sells at-cost but does **not**
   currently support `.fm`, so use **Porkbun** (clean UX, free WHOIS privacy) or
   **101domain/Namecheap** for the .fm, and keep Cloudflare Registrar in mind for any
   supporting `.com` (e.g., a defensive `getforay.com` / `forayapp.com`, ~$10–12/yr
   at cost) since foray.com itself is held by Foray Technologies.
2. **Reserve the handles the same day**: App Store Connect app name "Foray" (claimable
   at first app submission — names aren't reservable indefinitely, so don't dawdle once
   an Apple Developer account exists), plus @foray / @foray.fm on the socials that
   matter.
3. **USPTO filing is optional at this stage** — common-law rights begin with use, and
   the budget-conscious path is to file an intent-to-use application (TEAS Plus,
   **$350/class**) in Class 9 (downloadable software) and/or Class 42 (SaaS) once the
   name survives step 4. Not urgent pre-launch; cheap insurance pre-marketing-spend.
4. **Commission the formal clearance search before public launch**: USPTO TESS +
   state marks + live App Store/Play Store listings for "Foray" in software/audio
   classes. This study is a collision scan, not clearance (see disclaimer at top).
